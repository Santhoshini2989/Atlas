# Atlas Product Goals

## Purpose

This document defines the goals and boundaries of Atlas.

The purpose is to guide product decisions and prevent unnecessary complexity.

---

# Product Mission

Help software teams transform ideas into organized execution plans using AI assistance.

---

# Primary Goals

## Goal 1 — Improve Task Planning

### Objective

Help teams convert high-level ideas into clear, actionable tasks.

### Success Indicators

Users can:

- Create tasks faster.
- Generate better task descriptions.
- Understand requirements more clearly.

---

## Goal 2 — Reduce Information Fragmentation

### Objective

Keep important project context organized in one place.

### Success Indicators

Users can easily find:

- Project information.
- Task history.
- Decisions.
- Documentation.

---

## Goal 3 — Improve Project Visibility

### Objective

Help teams understand project progress.

### Success Indicators

Users can answer:

- What is happening?
- What is completed?
- What is blocked?
- What should happen next?

---

## Goal 4 — Provide Useful AI Assistance

### Objective

Use AI to remove repetitive project management work.

### Success Indicators

AI helps with:

- Task breakdown.
- Documentation generation.
- Summaries.
- Planning assistance.

---

# Non-Goals

A professional product also defines what it will NOT do.

## Not Building a Full Communication Platform

Atlas will not replace:

- Slack
- Email
- Video meetings

Communication features will only support project workflows.

---

## Not Replacing Developers

AI will assist teams.

AI will not:

- Automatically make all decisions.
- Replace engineering judgment.
- Write entire products without review.

---

## Not Building Everything Immediately

We will avoid:

- Too many integrations.
- Complex enterprise features.
- Advanced automation before validation.

---

# MVP Goals

The first version of Atlas should prove:

1. Teams can manage projects.
2. Teams can organize tasks.
3. AI can improve planning workflows.

---

# Product Decision Principles

When considering a feature, ask:

## Does it solve a real user problem?

If no:

Do not build it.

---

## Does it support the mission?

If no:

Delay it.

---

## Is it the simplest useful solution?

Prefer simplicity.

---

# Long-Term Goals

Eventually Atlas should become:

"The intelligent workspace where software teams plan, build, and ship products."

Future possibilities:

- AI project assistant.
- Engineering knowledge base.
- Automated documentation.
- Development workflow intelligence.
- Team productivity insights.
